A Neighbourhood’s Income's Impact on Environmental Engagement

Hypothesis

Neighbourhoods with higher average income levels show higher levels of environmental engagement such as recycling or participation in green initiatives, compared to neighbourhoods with lower average income levels.

What the Project is About

For this project, I am trying to find out if there is a correlation between the income of a neighbourhood in New York and the level of environmental engagement. In order to do this, I created different visualisation to show different aspect of the question.

Visualisation

This visualisation represents the different environmental programs in New York City. We can directly see that the most used program is "refuse" which stands for regular garbage.

The two visualisations on top top represents the Environmental engagement across NYC neighbourhood. The first one is more specific and as for the second one, we can see that Queens has a higher percentage of engagement across New York City.

This visualisations represents the Environmental Engagement by Income Level. I divided it by low, medium and high income because I wanted to see if there was a correlation between the Environmental engagement and the income.

Conclusion

In conclusion, we can say that environmental engagement increases with incomes. The trendlines show a strong positive relationship. There is a clear positive relationship between income level and environmental engagement in NYC. Higher-income households are more efficient at properly sorting recyclables, while lower-income groups experience more contamination and missed recycling opportunities.
